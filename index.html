Background

My Ph.D. thesis research focuses on the design of "Energy-aware (adaptive) and Energy-efficient (low-power) Protocols for Reliable Networking in Energy-Harvesting-Powered Systems for Structural Health Monitoring".

Throughout my years in the NeEWS Lab at MSU, I have worked on a number of projects in the Wireless Networked Sensing Systems and Internet of Things (IoT) domain for diverse applications.

These cover the gamut of human interaction measurement, structural health monitoring, environmental monitoring and control, quantification of structured task learning efficacy and cognitive flexibility, intelligent memory augmentation for cognitively-impaired patients etc.

I also enjoy working in teams and have collaborated with a diverse range of peers (across departments, universities, locations, nationalities) in both my work and research.
